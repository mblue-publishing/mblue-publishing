## Description

You have been out of work for one or more years and are now ready to return. Your last three applications went nowhere. No rejection email. No acknowledgement. Nothing. That is not bad luck. It is the Applicant Tracking System (ATS), and it reached its verdict before a human read your name.

The ATS now sits between you and every role you apply for. It scans your CV, registers the employment gap, and drops your score below the threshold. The recruiter never sees you. You get nothing back, and without knowing why, you conclude the problem is you.

It is not you. It is the system. And the system is navigable once you can see how it works.
Sarah Briggs spent fifteen years on the hiring side of this process. She built applicant tracking systems for major employers, trained recruiters, and sat on the panels that decided who got through. Then she took two and a half years out of the workforce to care for her mother, and found herself applying for jobs through the same systems she had helped design. This book is everything she learned from being on both sides of the table.

It is written for every type of career break, every gap length, and every sector. Childcare, elder care, health, burnout. One year out or seven. Finance, technology, HR, operations, law. The system is the same. So are the strategies for getting through it.

This is not generic job-search advice repackaged in returner language. It is a specific guide to the specific systems you need to navigate, written by someone who built those systems professionally and then had to beat them personally.
