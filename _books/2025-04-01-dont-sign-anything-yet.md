## Book Description

Nearly one in three women leave a grey divorce with no share of their spouse's retirement. Not because they weren't entitled to it. Because no one told them what the paperwork had to say before they signed it.

The pension, the 401(k), the survivor benefits: these were the other person's department for twenty or thirty years. That was a reasonable arrangement inside a marriage. Inside a settlement, it is a problem you now have weeks to fix.

The reference handbooks on pension division and QDRO law are written for attorneys and priced for law firm libraries. Nothing readable, practical, and built for the woman with a signing date approaching has existed.

Until now.

Don't Sign Anything Yet is a plain-English guide to claiming every retirement dollar you're owed before your grey divorce is final. Written by Diane Merritt, a Certified Divorce Financial Analyst with 18 years of family law experience and a grey divorce of her own, this book is organized around the decisions you face in the order you'll face them. Not a topic list. Your actual settlement timeline.

Diane knows this territory from both sides of the desk. At 53, two days before signing her own settlement, she found the survivor benefit language missing from her QDRO draft. She found it in time. Most women never look. This book makes sure you know where to look and what you're looking for.

You will learn how a defined benefit pension is calculated and why it cannot be compared to a 401(k) by account balance alone. You will understand what a QDRO must contain, which provisions are routinely absent from template orders, and what to do when something is missing before it becomes final. You will know your Social Security divorced-spouse entitlement and that no part of it is negotiable by the other side.

Every chapter ends with specific questions to bring to your next attorney meeting and a checklist of what must be verified before you move forward. Three appendices are designed to be printed and used alongside your actual documents.

The title was a warning. Now you have what you need to decide what to sign.

## Inside this book

- Why grey divorce settlements are financially different from other divorces and why the decisions made now are largely irreversible
- What a complete retirement asset disclosure should include and which plan types are routinely hidden, forgotten, or omitted from standard forms
- How a defined benefit pension is valued using the coverture fraction method and why you need an independent valuation before agreeing to any asset trade
- What a QDRO is, why it must be filed as a separate court order distinct from the divorce decree, and what happens to women who discover years later that theirs was never correctly submitted
- The six most common QDRO drafting errors, including the one that renders the entire order useless even after the plan administrator approves it
- Why survivor benefit language is absent from most template QDROs, what it must specifically say to protect you before and after he retires, and why this is the provision you cannot fix once the order is final
- How your Social Security divorced-spouse benefit is calculated, why claiming it requires no negotiation with the other side, and what the 10-year marriage threshold means for your filing timeline
- A structured 30-minute pre-signing protocol with attorney questions and document checks you can bring to the meeting where you are scheduled to sign
